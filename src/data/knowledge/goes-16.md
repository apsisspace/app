GOES-16 (originally GOES-R) is a NOAA geostationary weather satellite, the
first of the GOES-R series. It launched on 19 November 2016 aboard an Atlas V
from Cape Canaveral and operated as "GOES East" at 75.2° W longitude from
December 2017, replacing GOES-13. In early 2025 GOES-19 was commissioned
as the operational GOES East, and GOES-16 was moved to an on-orbit standby
position.

At geostationary altitude (~35,786 km over the equator), GOES-16 keeps pace
with Earth's rotation and stares continuously at the Americas and adjacent
ocean basins. Its Advanced Baseline Imager (ABI) produces full-disk imagery
every 10 minutes in 16 spectral bands, and its Geostationary Lightning
Mapper (GLM) provides the first operational continuous lightning detection
from GEO.

GOES-16 data is a backbone of U.S. hurricane, severe-weather, and
aviation-weather forecasting. Its five-minute mesoscale sectors are widely
used during rapidly developing convective events, and its space-environment
instruments feed NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center.

Designed for ~15 years of service, GOES-16 is expected to remain an active
on-orbit spare through the end of the decade.
