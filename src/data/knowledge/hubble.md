The Hubble Space Telescope is a 2.4-metre optical/UV/NIR observatory jointly
operated by NASA and ESA. It launched on STS-31 aboard Space Shuttle Discovery
on 24 April 1990 and orbits at roughly 540 km altitude with a 28.5° inclination.

Hubble's early spherical aberration was corrected by astronauts during Servicing
Mission 1 (STS-61, December 1993), and four subsequent shuttle servicing
missions upgraded instruments and reaction wheels, most recently SM4 in May
2009. No further crewed servicing is possible now that the Shuttle is retired.

Hubble has produced some of the most scientifically and culturally important
astronomical images of the modern era — the Deep Fields, the Pillars of
Creation, and direct observations of exoplanet atmospheres among them. Its
observations have been cited in tens of thousands of peer-reviewed papers.

The telescope continues to operate in a degraded-but-functional mode as of
2025. Its orbit is slowly decaying; without a reboost it is expected to
re-enter the atmosphere some time in the mid-to-late 2030s. NASA and private
partners have studied reboost missions but none is currently funded.
