Starlink-1007 (NORAD 44713) is one of sixty v1.0 satellites launched on the
"Starlink v1.0 L1" mission — the first fully operational Starlink batch —
on 11 November 2019 from Cape Canaveral SLC-40 aboard a Falcon 9. It is
representative of the early operational Starlink shell, orbiting near 550 km
altitude at 53° inclination.

Each v1.0 satellite masses ~260 kg, uses krypton-fuelled Hall-effect
thrusters for station-keeping and deorbit, and carries phased-array antennas
for Ku-band user links plus Ka-band gateway links. Unlike later v1.5 and v2
Mini designs, v1.0 satellites do not carry laser inter-satellite links.

SpaceX's Starlink constellation has grown from this first batch to well over
6,000 active satellites serving millions of customers worldwide, with
continued launches of v1.5 and v2 Mini vehicles on Falcon 9 and v2 full
vehicles planned for Starship. Older v1.0 satellites are being gradually
deorbited — their propellant and attitude control gear wear out, and the
network is designed for rapid refresh.

Starlink satellites have driven significant discussion in the astronomy
community about orbital brightness, radio-frequency interference, and the
scale of megaconstellations.
